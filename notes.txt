EDA Conclusion:
Exploratory Data Analysis Summary

The dataset contains 395 students and 33 features, including academic performance indicators, demographic variables, and behavioral attributes.

Target Variable :
A binary risk indicator was created based on the final grade:
- Risk = 1 if G3 < 10 (student fails)
- Risk = 0 otherwise

The class distribution is:
- Safe students: 67%
- At-risk students: 33%

The dataset therefore presents moderate class imbalance, which remains acceptable for standard classification models.

Key Predictive Variables :
Exploratory analysis revealed strong relationships between previous academic performance and final grade.
The strongest correlations with G3 were:
Variable	Correlation
G2		0.90
G1		0.80
failures	-0.36
These results indicate that previous academic performance is the most important predictor of final success.
Students with higher grades in earlier periods (G1, G2) are significantly more likely to succeed.

Behavioral and Socioeconomic Factors :
Some variables showed weaker relationships with academic success:
Variable	Correlation
Medu		0.21
Fedu		0.15
studytime	0.09
These factors may contribute marginally to predictive performance but are less influential than academic history.

Weak Predictors :
Variables such as absences and freetime showed very low correlation with final grade, suggesting limited predictive power when considered individually.

Key Insight : 
The analysis suggests that academic trajectory variables (G1 and G2) dominate the prediction of final performance, while lifestyle and social variables play a secondary role.
